Tips for Keeping Your Home Safe During COVID-19 Pandemic


Tips for Keeping Your Home Safe:

Ensure that reusable clothes are disinfected or washed at 60C after every use. Try to use a disposable cloth or paper towels as much as possible. Safeguard against service people. Disinfect the Common Surfaces and dried after use. Curtains can be cleaned or disinfected by steam cleaning.


Bathroom / toilet

Use toilet cleaners and brushes at regular intervals.
If you live in an area where water hardening is a problem, make sure that you remove the limescale using the descaling product. Disinfect toilet seat, handle, and rim regularly.
If they are used regularly, clean bath and sink frequently.
If they have been used by someone who is ill, use a disinfectant.
Clean Shower Curtains Regularly.

Kitchen

Food preparation surfaces should be cleaned before and after use.
Use different chopping boards for unbaked food – such as raw meat – and food that does not need cooking, such as salad leaves.
Wash and dry your hands after handling raw meat.

Domestic Animal

Keep pet food away from human food.
Wash your hands after touching animals and their food, toys, cages, and litter trays.
Bowls used for pet food must be stored separately.

Laundry

Wash your hands after handling dirty laundry
All underwear, towels, and household linen should be washed with a bleach-based laundry product at 60C or 40C to prevent germs from spreading.
Do not wash clothes in the washing machine as germs can multiply rapidly.

Waste Disposal

Foot bins are more hygienic as they reduce the risk of germs on your hands.
Always wash your hands after handling useless materials.
Throw away rubbish carefully to avoid attracting vermin and insects.
